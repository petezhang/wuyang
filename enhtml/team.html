Austenitic Stainless Steel

View details

Product introduction:

Austenitic stainless steel, refers to the stainless steel with a stable austenite at high temperature and room temperature. It contain Cr about 18%, Ni 8% ~ 10%, C at 0.1%,. Austenitic chromium-nickel stainless steel including the famous steel 18Cr-8Ni and base on that increase the component of Cr, Ni and adding Mo, Cu, Si, Nb, Ti and other elements developed series of high Cr-Ni steel. the non-magnetic austenitic stainless steel are of high toughness and ductility, but lower strength, it is difficult to make it harden through the phase transition, it can only be strengthened by cold working, such as adding S, Ca, Se, Te and other elements, it has good cutting resistance, excellent corrosion resistance and workability and plasticity which result in its wide range of applications.

Super Austenitic Stainless Steel

View details

Product introduction:

When the resistance to pitting corrosion of chromium-nickel austenitic stainless steel of equivalent value (PREN = [Cr] +3.3 [Mo] +16 [N]) ≥40%, can be called super austenitic stainless steel. Super austenitic stainless steel is mainly to solve the corrosion under harsh conditions including inadequate thermal stability of the organization. Its resistance to pitting, crevice corrosion resistance is excellent. the mechanical properties of super austenitic stainless steel is also of good performance of high yield strength and good workability

(Super) Dual Phase Stainless Steel Tube

View details

Product introduction:

Duplex stainless steel is featured with α + γ two-phase structure, so its present both features of austenitic stainless steel and ferritic stainless steel, it's the best stainless stee ofl high strength and corrosion resistance. Typically, in the case of containing low C, Cr content will be 18% ~ 28%, Ni content of 3% to 10%. Some steel further contains Mo, Cu, Nb, Ti, N and other alloying elements. Compared with the ferrite, it's with more plasticity, higher toughness, brittle at room temperature, both resistance to intergranular corrosion and welding performance were significantly improved, while also maintaining a ferritic stainless steel 475 ℃ brittleness and high thermal conductivity. Compared with the austenitic stainless steel, which has excellent resistance to general corrosion and resistance to localized corrosion, particularly resistance to stress corrosion, pitting corrosion, crevice corrosion, corrosion fatigue and abrasion, etc.

Ferritic Stainless Steel

View details

Product introduction:

Ferritic stainless steel is a type of chromium alloy that has ferritic structure at elevated and room temperature with body-centered cubic crystal structures. The chemical composition consisting of C≤0.02% and Cr 10.5%~32% and cannot be hardened by heat treatment. Ferritic stainless steel is used in comprehensive applications with excellent general properties, and is taken as a kind of primary nickel-saving stainless steels.

Titanium Alloys

View details

Product introduction:

Titanium alloys are commonly divided into three groups according to the type of crystal structure: 1. Ti with Al, Sn; 2. Ti with Al, Cr, Mo, V; 3. Ti with Al, V. They have good mechanical and punching properties and can be welded by various forms of welding procedures with welded joint strength up to 90% of base metal. Titanium is capable of corrosion resistance to chlorides, sulfides and ammonia, and has better corrosion resistance than aluminum, stainless steel and nickel alloy for seawater service

Main grades:

Grade1, Grade2, Grade5, Grade9etc.
